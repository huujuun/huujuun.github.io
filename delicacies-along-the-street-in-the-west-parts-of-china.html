Delicacies along the Street in the west parts of China

Sep 29, 2019 • huujuun

Sichuan Malatang

Malatang seems to be more of a magnet than most other highly addictive street foods. Most likely it’s because of the number of choices you get. For Malatang, the ingredients are placed on thin sticks like kebabs. When a customer comes, thevendor will dip the skewered food into the boiling bittern, season them with capsicum powder and seed powder of Chinese prickly ash, and hand the kebab to the customer, who then will eat while walking. This kind of take -away xiaochi has now been introduced into restaurants, evolving into Chuanchuanxiang with hot pot characteristics, a new generation of Malatang.

Xi'an Xiaochi

angroupaomo is one of the signature dishes of “Xi’an”. The tender slices of mutton can easily fill up your stomach in minutes. But the aftertaste of the mutton soup can last for hours. Biangbiangmian is a type of noodle popular in China’s Shanxi Province. The noodles, touted as one of the ten strange wonders of “Shanxi”, are described as being like a belt, due to their thickness and length.

Malatang and Chinese film culture

Contemporary urban film “Aiqing Malatang” , portraying modern love and lifestyle in Beijing, splashed onto Chinese cinema screens in 1997. The film is an anthology of sorts, and tells its story of love and life in modern Beijing through a series of vignettes. Made on a budget of just three million Yuan, the movie grossed almost 30 million yuan at the Chinese box office, second only to that year’s blockbuster, Titanic. “Aiqing Malatang” starts with a young couple eating a two-sided hot pot, shaped in a yin and yang pattern. Until the couple’s wedding at the end of the film “Aiqing Malatang” intermittently shows Six different episodes about different generations’ relationships. Lovecan be sweet, sour or spicy, which can all be tasted in this contemporary Chinese film “Aiqing Malatang”.
